My experience with GNU/Linux

[2023-02-06]

My experience with GNU/Linux has been weird ride, I have been using these systems for approximately a year and a half. I fell in love with them, since the day I heard about them. When I first downloaded Ubuntu and started using it, I could feel that It is just different and I like different. The power of the terminal was just perfect. I loved it. I started looking more into it, and as I kept diving deeper, I realised there is much more to it.

Soon, I discovered the reddit page /r/unixporn, and it blew my mind. I thought to myself, is this really possible, to customize your desktop to that level. So, I started learning about ricing. I had to discover all of this by myself, so it took some time, but I was never afraid to break my computer, so I just went for it. Honestly, It was the best choice, because I learnt a lot. I moved to ArchLinux and tried many other distros but only some of them were my fit.

But, not everything is fun, I even saw the sophisticated, elitist, and toxic side of this community and slowly hated it. It started to change me, but after a while, I understood and chose my own path.

Now, I follow my own path. All my computers use Linux. I use the distribution - PopOS by System76. I use this distribution, because It has everything I need. I don't want to dive deeper into this right now, but try it for yourself and you'll know.

I'm getting better with shell scripting, and I'm planning to do a mini project with shell soon, keep tabs on the projects section for updates.
